Construction

Not just a product, it's an integrated platform. Hidden beneath the minimalist Kangaroo shell is one of the most complicated ready-to-wear fashion garments currently in manufacture. The Series Zero jackets are constructed as two separate garments with diametrically opposing seams, augmenting one another to maximize protection and performance. The two garments are expertly refitted in the final stage.

Armor

Hidden below the Kangaroo exterior, the Vectran "Core" layer provides a significant level of augmented protection. Within our garments, Vectran is attached with seams diametrical to the Kangaroo exterior layout. Acting as a redundant system separate from the exterior, the Vectran “Core” is our primary protective layer. Based on our materials research we're expecting a 7+ second abrasion time added to our garments. It also protects you from material and seam bursts, cuts and punctures. We apply our Vectran layer in a manner which exceeds all product safety requirements.

Standards

All Series Zero garments are designed to meet the highest standard of protective apparel in the world -- CE EN 13595:1-2012 VO6, the French/European Union's safety Standard for Personal Protective Equipment. Due to its rigorously high standard, most brands choose to forego this level of testing. In fact, since its inception in 2002, only a handful of products have ever been certified to legally use the term PPE (Personal Protective Equipment).
